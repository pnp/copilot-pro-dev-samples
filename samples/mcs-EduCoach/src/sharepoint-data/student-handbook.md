# Contoso University — Student Handbook

## Graduation Requirements

To graduate with a Bachelor of Science degree, students must complete a minimum of **120 credits** with a cumulative GPA of at least **2.0**.

Credits are distributed across the following requirement categories:

| Category | Credits Required |
|---|---|
| General Education | 30 |
| Major Required | 45 |
| Major Electives | 15 |
| Free Electives | 30 |
| **Total** | **120** |

All Major Required courses must be completed with a grade of C or higher. General Education requirements may be satisfied by transfer credit with prior approval from the registrar.

## Academic Policies

### Prerequisites

Prerequisites must be satisfied before enrolling in a course. A grade of D or higher satisfies a prerequisite unless the course description states otherwise. Students who enrol without meeting prerequisites will be administratively withdrawn.

### Credit Load

The standard full-time credit load is 15 credits per semester (5 courses). Students wishing to take more than 18 credits must obtain academic advisor approval. Students on academic probation may not exceed 12 credits.

### Incomplete Grades

A grade of I (Incomplete) may be assigned when a student cannot complete coursework due to documented extenuating circumstances. The incomplete must be resolved within 8 weeks of the following semester. Unresolved incompletes convert to an F.

### Academic Probation

Students whose cumulative GPA falls below 2.0 are placed on academic probation. Students on probation must meet with their academic advisor within the first two weeks of the following semester.

### Grade Appeals

Grade appeals must be submitted within 30 days of the grade posting. The appeal process begins with the course instructor, then the department chair, and finally the Academic Standards Committee.

## Enrollment and Registration

### Registration Windows

- **Priority registration** opens 6 weeks before the semester start for students with 90+ credits
- **Standard registration** opens 4 weeks before the semester start for all other students
- **Late registration** (with a $50 fee) is available until the end of the second week of the semester

### Waitlisting

Students may join a course waitlist if a section is full. Waitlist positions are offered in order. Students on a waitlist receive a Teams notification when a seat becomes available and have 24 hours to accept. If the offer expires, the next waitlisted student is notified.

### Course Withdrawal

Students may withdraw from a course without academic penalty until the **8th week** of the semester. After week 8, withdrawal requires dean's approval and results in a W grade on the transcript. W grades do not affect GPA but count toward attempted credits.

## Degree Audit

A degree audit shows your progress toward graduation. It lists completed credits by category, remaining requirements, and your projected graduation term. Degree audits are updated nightly.

To request an official degree audit, contact the registrar at registrar@contosouniversity.edu or visit the Registrar's Office in Building A, Room 105.

## Special Accommodations

Students requiring academic accommodations due to a disability should contact the Office of Accessibility Services (OAS) before the semester begins. Accommodations are not retroactive.

## Academic Advising

Every student is assigned a primary academic advisor based on their declared major. Advising appointments are available in-person (Building B, Room 210) or via Teams. Walk-in hours are Monday and Wednesday, 2–4 PM.

For questions about prerequisites, degree requirements, course availability, or enrollment, EduCoach is available 24 hours a day via Microsoft Teams.

For complex situations including petition processes, special exceptions, transfer credit evaluation, or academic appeals, please schedule an appointment with your assigned advisor.
